Australian Standards Notes:
  • AS/NZS 3000:2018 cl. 3.9.4 — Cable trays shall be sized so that cables can be installed and removed without damage. Cables shall be laid in a single layer where practicable.
  • AS/NZS 3000:2018 cl. 3.9.5 — Cable trays shall have adequate strength to support the weight of cables plus a minimum working load of 1 kN applied at mid-span.
  • AS/NZS 3000:2018 cl. 3.9.7 — Segregation: Extra-low voltage (ELV) cables must be physically separated from LV cables or have an insulating barrier.
  • AS 3013:2005 cl. 2.3 — Standard tray widths: 75, 100, 150, 225, 300, 450, 600, 750, 900 mm. Standard depths: 25, 50, 75, 100 mm.
  • AS/NZS 3008.1.1 Tables 22–23 — Derating factors apply for grouped cables on cable trays. Single-layer touching ≈ Table 22, multi-layer ≈ Table 23.
  • AS/NZS 3000:2018 cl. 3.9.3 — Minimum bend radius must be maintained at tray fittings.
  • Vertical risers — Cables must be secured by cleats at max 300 mm centres (or per manufacturer). The tray fill shall not exceed 40% of the usable cross-sectional area.
  • Fire rating — Where cable trays pass through fire-rated barriers, fire stopping per AS 1530.4 / AS 4072 is required.
